who are us

CairoHacks is Cairo’s next big thing! For 48 hours along three days students will gather to share ideas, build great tech projects and meet industry experts. We will provide workshops, mentorship and all that’s needed for a great stay. Come join us for a weekend full of innovation, learning and building projects that can change our society

Our Mission

CairoHacks aims to be the first student-run annual hackathon that offers an unmatched environment for anyone interested in tech to share ideas and shape Egypt’s future tech scene.

FAQ

A: Any undergraduate student or a student who graduated in 2018 is more than welcome to attend!

Q2: Are there any fees to attend CairoHacks?

A: No, the event is completely free of charge..

Q3:I want to come but I don’t have a team, can I come alone?

A: Absolutely! There will be team formation session where participants can can connect with new people and form their own team!

A: No, Participants can build their idea after they arrive and gain knowledge from fellow students or mentors.

    WHAT IS CAIRO HACKS?

    Cairohacks is a 3-Day Hackathon organized to gather ambitious students from all over Egypt and empower them to learn, communicate and innovate. We provide participants with Mentorship, Workshops and purposive tools.

    MISSION

    Our Goal is to improve Egypt’s and the World’s utilities and needs in various tracks,  by encouraging the participants to innovate. We aim to release the potential of students and make them bring the best outcome, as well as communicating and getting to know each other, which helps in forming start-ups and communities.

    We aim to give students the perfect atmosphere for learning, releasing their best potential and building tech projects that are innovative and solve real problems. We aim to bridge the gap between academia and industry and help student learn technical and business skills.

    DEFINITIONS

    Hackathon: An Invention Marathon, typically lasting several days, in which developers meet to engage in collaborative computer programming.

    The Participants:  Any student or fresh 2018 graduate who competes in CairoHacks.

    The Organisers:  CairoHacks team, who are students from the Faculty of Computer and information science at Ain Shams University

    The Attendee:  Refers to anyone participating in the event without competing. Such as judges, sponsors, mentors, workshop moderators, etc

    CLAUSES

    Competition Rules:

    ⦁ Participating teams can have from 3 to 5 members.
    ⦁ All of the members must be students (undergraduates) or fresh 2018 graduates.
    ⦁ All of the work done on the team’s project must be initiated during the event.
    ⦁ All of the team members have to be present in the event.
    ⦁ Members are allowed to work on their projects at night between event days.
    ⦁ Teams can’t use any assets that they had created before the event, though teams can use an idea they had before the event.
    ⦁ Teams can’t use other people’s work and present it.
    ⦁ Teams can work on an idea that they had worked on before as long as they don’t re-use any of the assets or code.
    ⦁ Participants can use frameworks, libraries, and open-source tools in their projects. But, using resources that aren't equally available to everyone isn’t allowed.
    ⦁ Teams are allowed to add features to existing projects, but the team points will be determined only according to the new features and functionalities.
    ⦁ Teams must stop coding as soon as the time is up! Only Minor details or couple of lines of code that fix a certain bug can be adjusted after the time is up.
    ⦁ Projects that don’t follow the code of conduct or any rules stated in this document may be exempted from the competition.
    ⦁ After the time is up, each team should present a demo showcasing their project to the judges and the attendees.
    ⦁ Misbehavior, violence, or offensive behavior towards any other attendee or participant won’t be tolerated and appropriate action will be taken according to the situation.
    Such misbehaviours can lead to disqualifying the offender immediately.
    ⦁ Ideas or actions that may carry any offence towards a certain person or group, or ideas/actions that don’t follow Egypt Laws can’t and won’t be accepted.
    ⦁ Cheating or stealing another team’s source code is forbidden.

    CODE OF CONDUCT

    At CairoHacks we believe that every participant/attendee has the right to attend the event safely and feel safe and welcomed.

    ⦁ Harassment is not tolerated in any way, that includes verbal abuse or offensively written comments relating to gender, beliefs, physical image, social class, race, country of origin, economic status, or any other personal features/characteristics.

    ⦁ Participants are expected to immediately stop if they are asked to stop any harassing behaviour, that includes making any other participant uncomfortable.

    ⦁ Everyone at CairoHacks are subject to the anti-harassment policy, including participants, judges, mentors, volunteers, organisers, sponsors, and the staff of the BUE.

    ⦁ Participants are not allowed to include any sexualised material or activities, anything too offensive, racist/culturally insensitive material, overly political or violent speech or scenery during the event. This also applies to their clothing, projects, and assets used

    If the participant feels harassed or witnesses someone feeling uncomfortable or harassed, they are to report immediately to the CairoHacks staff. Event organisers will be available to help the participant/attendee with their situations to ensure the highest quality experience for them.
